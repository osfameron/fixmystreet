Frequently Asked Questions

What is the City of Readers Map App?

We have devised this map so everyone in the city can take part in City of Readers. We want you to share your:

  • book groups
  • favourite reading spots
  • reading events
  • school / library / community centre activities and
  • literary facts about liverpool
This is very easy to do - simply go to 'tell us a story' and follow the simple steps.

Why not look up other events, activities and facts while you're here? Simply go to 'all stories' and click on the map or use the drop down list.

Need Help?
Contact Emma Melling.
Who is this project run by?
See the City of Readers website for full details!
What software is running this site?
This map uses FixMyStreet by mySociety. FixMyStreet is a site to help people report, view, or discuss local problems they’ve found to their local council by simply locating them on a map. It launched in early February 2007.
The City of Readers mapping project uses the core functionality, but with customised wordings, to talk about stories and events instead of potholes!
How do I use the site?
After entering a postcode or location, you are presented with a map of that area. You can view stories already reported in that area, or tell ones of your own simply by clicking on the map at the location of the problem.
Is it free?
The site is free to use, yes.

The website should work on your mobile phone or tablet, adapting to the size of your screen automatically.

Practical Questions

Do you remove silly or illegal content?
City of Readers is not responsible for the content and accuracy of material submitted by its users. We reserve the right to edit or remove any problems or updates which we consider to be inappropriate upon being informed by a user of the site.
Why does the site use kilometres for measurements?
Thanks for asking politely – we never quite understand why some of the rudest emails we receive are on this topic. The British national grid reference system, devised by Ordnance Survey (the British national mapping agency) around the time of the second world war, uses eastings and northings measured in metres and kilometres; the maps we use are from Ordnance Survey and so this is what we use to display distances. There you have it: not everything British is in miles!

Privacy Questions

Who gets to see my email address?
If you submit a problem, we pass on your details, and details of the story, to the City of Readers team. Only people we authorise to view the administration interface will be able to see your email address and they will never use it for anything other than to help administer the map. Similarly with email addresses from updates. We will never give or sell your email address to anyone else, unless we are obliged to by law. Your name will not be published anywhere unless you let us.
Will you send nasty, brutish spam to my email address?
Never. We will email you if someone leaves an update on a story you’ve reported. We will only ever send you emails in relation to your story.
